Mario's Italian Ice Review
Mauricio Gonzalez
Columbia University in The City of New York Rising Senior

If you ever find yourself in Chicago and are looking for a delicious refreshing treat, you can not pass up on Mario's Italian Ice!

Mario's Italian Lemonade was established in 1954 by Mario DiPaolo and his son, Mario G. DiPaolo, also known as “Skip.” At the age of six, Skip DiPaolo was a very rambunctious child with endless energy--his father gave him a hand-crank lemonade machine to channel this energy by making and selling lemonade. Skip DiPaolo churned the hand-crank machine and sold lemonade for two cents on Taylor Street. Within the next few years, Skip's father built what is now known as “Mario's Italian Lemonade.” During the early years, the lemonade stand was run by Skip, his sister (Donna) his mother (Dorothy) and his father, Mario. It's a small little stand located in Little Italy - 1068 W Taylor St, Chicago, IL 60607.

What makes Mario's Italian Ice so special is that they use natural ingredients rather than artifical sweetners!
That is something that I personally look out for as I am trying to fix my diet and eat healthier than I have in the past.

It was 80 degrees outside and this was the perfect treat to cool off and have a unique desert that is healthy and tasty!

I can not stress enough how good this place is! If you are ever in town or visiting, you must put this on top of your to do list!
